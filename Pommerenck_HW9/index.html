The Bronze Age Collapse

The Bronze Age Collapse was a period of time between approximately 1250 BC and 1100 BC during which occurred a widespread societal collapse, with many relatively independent civilizations entering sharp declines or even vanishing altogether.
The exact reasons for this period in history is a subject of widespread debate, covering everything from disease, famine, volcanic eruptions, earthquake storms, and the mysterious "Sea-Peoples".

This Webpage will cover the following subjects related to the Bronze Age Collapse:

  • Notable Civilizations affected by the collapse.
  • A number of theories about what caused the collapse.
  • A discussion about separating historical facts from the myths of the time.
  • A look at theories surrounding the Sea-Peoples and who they may have been.
  • A look at what effects the collapse had on history moving forwards.
  • Additional resources used in the construction of this webpage and jumping off points for further research into the subject.
